Frequently Asked Questions

What is the currency used on Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Currency exchange services are available at banks and major hotels.

What are the power outlet specifications?

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States. Visitors from other countries may need a voltage converter or plug adapter.

Is English widely spoken?

English is widely spoken in urban areas and tourist spots, especially by younger Tanitians. In rural areas, especially among older residents, English may be less common.

How do I get around the island?

Public buses serve Taniti City, and private buses cover the rest of the island. Taxis and rental cars are available, and the island is bike-friendly with rental services offering bikes and helmets.

Are there any important local laws I should be aware of?

Yes, alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m., and helmets are required when riding bikes. Also, be mindful of national holidays when businesses may be closed.
